Big-O

There isn't really much to say about Big-O.
Yes, the full process of amortization for clock cycles could be covered,
But the entire point of the nomenclature is to avoid all that heady lifting.
Unless of course, you are trying to go off rez and need to proof your solutions.

The goal is to be efficient.
Less time spent. Less space used.
As with all things, tradeoffs will happen.
But in general, from best to worst,
We aim for space or time complexity of:

  1. O(1) Constant Algorithms of this such take the same amount of time, usually quite small, regardless of the number of inputs.
  2. O(log n) Logarithmic Grows, but very slowly, with the number of inputs.
  3. O(n) Linear Grows in size at the same rate of input growth
  4. O(n log n) nlogn Grows very fast
  5. O(n^2) Quadratic Grows very very fast. Rule of Thumb: nested loops almost always land here...
  6. O(n^3) And things only get worse from here.

Problem Solving:

Pretty common sense stuff here too.

  1. Make sure you understand the problem. The perfect answer to the wrong question has little marketable value.
  2. Find a concrete example. Nothing comes from Nothing. Relate or Locate an example to enhance understanding
  3. Break It Down. Make note of your path
  4. Code! Or continue breaking it down if you can't figure out a bit
  5. Optimize. Yes, this is a final, and often optional, step. Having something that works is more useful than having nothing.
